Copy Editing Samples

The Kansas City Star

Torrential storms dump up to 5 inches of rain in KC area, causing flash flooding

I was the nightside news editor when this flash flooding started. I worked very closely to communicate with the breaking news reporter, who was in the newsroom with me; staff photographers, who were collecting footage of flooding ; and news editors, who helped look for updates from authorities on local media. I produced this story with the goal of keeping readers informed on critical safety information. I collaborated with staff to update for road closures, attach videos, and embed flood warnings and images from Twitter.

NAACP issues first-ever travel advisory for a state — and it’s Missouri

While editing this, I took extra care to make sure the story was put in proper context. I made sure to link to KC Star stories about some of the hate incidents mentioned, and I included videos that ran with those stories as well to create a complete package. I wrote the headline and edited the metadata for this story, which were key elements that helped it perform exceptionally well on search engines — at one point if you searched "NAACP," this was the first news story to pop up.

A rosé by any other name would taste as dry

I edited this story for AP style, paying particular attention to the names of wines. I also wrote the headline and linked to some of the vintners the author mentions.

Twitter, we have a problem: Woman moves NASA shirts to girls' section at Target, sets internet abuzz

I edited and rearranged some paragraphs and embedded content in this story to make it flow narratively and visually. I also wrote the headline.

Therapeutic horse riding lets equestrians take the reins over their disabilities

While editing this story, I took extra care to use inclusive language when talking about disabilities. At one point a quote read that some of the clients were "confined to wheelchairs." I spoke with the news editor of this piece and we decided to leave that part of the quote out because wheelchairs give people with disabilities freedom to move rather than limit them.

Former MU swimmer Mack Darragh qualifies for 2016 Rio Olympics

While checking this June 13, 2016, article, it came to my attention that swimmer Mack Darragh couldn't have qualified for the 200-meter fly with his time in the trials. I brought it to the reporter's attention and the error was fixed the next day. I also reorganized the story to improve its flow.
